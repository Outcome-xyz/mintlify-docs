A market on Outcome is a question with a definitive answer and a price for each possible answer. You trade on probability: every outcome token is a contract that pays exactly **1 USDH** if its outcome wins and **0 USDH** if it doesn't. The current price is the market's collective probability estimate.

## How prices work

Outcome tokens are priced between **0** and **1** - interpret the price directly as a probability:

- A Yes priced at **0.65** implies the market thinks there's a **65% chance** the event happens.
- The matching No would price near **0.35** (binary outcomes roughly sum to 1, modulo spread and basis).
- Prices float freely between **0.0001** (near impossibility) and **0.9999** (near certainty) based on supply and demand on the orderbook.

Two corollaries fall out of this:

1. Buy a Yes at `p`, hold to a Yes resolution → realised return per token = `(1 − p) / p`.
2. Buy a Yes at `p`, hold to a No resolution → realised loss per token = `p`.

Your maximum profit per token is `1 − price`. Your maximum loss per token is `price`. No leverage, no liquidations - outcome tokens are pre-collateralised by their counterparty's offsetting share.

## Taking a position

| You believe                                   | Action                              |
| --------------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------- |
| Outcome is more likely than the price implies | **Buy Yes**                         |
| Outcome is less likely than the price implies | **Buy No**                          |
| The market overshot - sentiment will revert   | Sell at the new price before expiry |

You can sell at any time before expiry to lock in P&L. If you don't, your position settles automatically at resolution: winning tokens redeem for 1 USDH each, losing tokens expire worthless. There's no margin call, no early-close penalty - the only cost of holding is opportunity cost on the USDH locked in the position.

## Payouts at resolution

Every market resolves to exactly one winning outcome. At resolution:

- **Winning Yes tokens** redeem for **1 USDH** each.
- **Winning No tokens** of any outcome that _didn't_ win also resolve to 1 USDH each (every losing outcome's No is a winner - see the [HIP-4 protocol](/concepts/hip4) page for why).
- **Losing tokens** expire worthless.

This is why the bundle equivalence `Yes + No = 1` works: holding both legs of an outcome is equivalent to holding 1 USDH no matter what resolves.

## What you can trade

Outcome supports four market types - each with its own structure and trading rhythm. The full breakdown is in [Market types](/markets/types):

- **Recurring price binary** - auto-generated "BTC > $X by Y" markets, refreshed every 15m / 1h / 1d.
- **Labelled binary** - standalone Yes/No markets with custom side names ("Hypurr" vs "Usain Bolt").
- **Multi-outcome** - questions with three or more named outcomes plus a fallback.
- **Price bucket** - recurring multi-outcome markets across a sequence of price ranges.

For the lifecycle (open → trade → expire → resolve → settle), see [Market lifecycle](/markets/lifecycle).

## Network and execution

All trading happens on **Hyperliquid L1**. Orders match in the same on-chain orderbook that powers Hyperliquid perps:

- **Fast finality** - settlement in the L1 block, no confirmations to wait through.
- **No gas fees on trades** - only the Hyperliquid maker/taker spread applies.
- **Same authentication as perps** - agent keys sign every order silently after one-time approval.

For the full picture of how this all sits inside Hyperliquid, see [Hyperliquid](/concepts/hyperliquid-hip4).

Every market on Outcome represents a question with a definitive answer. You trade by buying tokens that represent your view — Yes if you think something will happen, No if you think it won't. Prices move between 0 and 1 as sentiment shifts, and when the market closes and resolves, one side wins and pays $1.00 per token.

## Market mechanics

### Binary outcomes

Every market resolves to exactly one winning outcome. In a Yes/No binary market, either Yes wins or No wins — there is no middle ground. Named markets work the same way: one named outcome is declared the winner, and the rest expire worthless.

### Token pricing

Outcome tokens are priced on a scale of 0 to 1, where the price directly represents the market-implied probability of that outcome winning:

- A Yes token priced at **0.65** implies a **65% probability** the event happens.
- A No token for the same market would be priced at approximately **0.35**, since probabilities on a binary market roughly sum to 1.
- Prices move freely between **0.00001** (nearly impossible) and **0.99999** (near certainty) based on supply and demand.

### Taking a position

- **Buy Yes** if you believe the outcome is more likely than the current price implies.
- **Buy No** if you believe the outcome is less likely than the current price implies.
- You can sell your tokens at any time before expiry to lock in a gain or cut a loss.

### Payouts at resolution

- If **Yes wins**: each Yes token pays out **$1.00 USDH**. No tokens expire worthless.
- If **No wins**: each No token pays out **$1.00 USDH**. Yes tokens expire worthless.

Your maximum profit per token is $1.00 minus what you paid. Your maximum loss per token is what you paid.

## Market lifecycle

<Steps>
  <Step title="Market opens">
    Trading begins with initial prices set by the market. You can start placing buy and sell orders immediately through the orderbook.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Price discovery">
    As traders take positions, the market price continuously reflects the collective probability estimate. More trading activity generally leads to tighter spreads and more reliable pricing.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Expiry">
    At the scheduled expiry time, the market stops accepting new orders and all open orders are cancelled. Your existing positions are locked in.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Resolution">
    The platform determines the winning outcome — for price binary markets, this is based on the oracle price at expiry. For named outcome markets, resolution depends on the real-world event result.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Settlement">
    Winning tokens become redeemable for exactly $1.00 USDH each. You can redeem them through the platform or they may be automatically settled, depending on market configuration.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Types of markets

### Recurring price binary markets

These markets are automatically created on a regular schedule and ask whether an asset price will be above or below a specific threshold at a specific time — for example, "BTC > $69,000 by 3pm." New versions open continuously (daily, hourly, etc.), so there is always an active market to trade on your short-term price outlook.

### Custom named outcome markets

These are manually created markets on specific events: elections, crypto governance votes, sports outcomes, or anything with a definitive result. Side names reflect the actual choices (for example, "Hypurr" vs. "Usain Bolt"). Custom markets can be binary or multi-outcome.

### Price bucket markets

Price bucket markets ask whether an asset will fall within a specific price range — for example, "Is BTC between $80,000 and $82,000 at expiry?" Rather than a simple above/below question, you're trading on a narrower band, which typically means higher potential payouts for correct predictions.

## Fee structure

Outcome uses a maker/taker model consistent with Hyperliquid's fee structure:

- **Makers** (limit orders that rest on the book and provide liquidity) pay lower fees.
- **Takers** (orders that immediately match against resting orders) pay higher fees.

Some interfaces may charge an optional builder fee on top of the base exchange fees. If a builder fee applies, it will be shown before you confirm your order.

<Note>
  All order execution happens on Hyperliquid L1. This means trades settle with fast finality and you pay no gas fees — the cost of trading is limited to the maker/taker spread and any applicable builder fees.
</Note>

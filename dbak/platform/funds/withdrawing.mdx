Withdrawals use the same two providers as deposits, in reverse. Pick how you want to receive funds, and Outcome handles the USDH → USDC swap on HyperCore spot, the cross-chain transfer to wherever the provider needs it, and the off-ramp itself.

## Two ways to withdraw

| Provider | What it handles | Destination |
|---|---|---|
| **Across** | Crypto withdrawals | Bridge USDC (or supported assets) to Ethereum, Arbitrum, Base, Optimism, Polygon, or any other supported chain. |
| **Meld** | Cash withdrawals | Off-ramp to bank account, card, or other fiat methods. |

You don't need to manually convert USDH to USDC, manually transfer between accounts, or manually trigger a Hyperliquid bridge withdrawal. Outcome composes those steps under the chosen provider.

## Before you withdraw

Cancel any open orders and close any positions you don't want to hold. Open orders lock USDH as collateral - if you try to withdraw while orders are pending, you may not have enough free balance to cover the full withdrawal.

<Warning>
  Cancel all open orders before starting the withdrawal flow. Outstanding orders reserve USDH from your balance, and reserved USDH isn't available to withdraw.
</Warning>

## The flow

<Steps>
  <Step title="Close positions and cancel open orders">
    From the **Positions** and **Orders** views, sell your outcome tokens (or use [merge outcome / merge question](/platform/trading/converting) where applicable) and cancel any resting orders. Wait for everything to settle into free USDH.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Pick a withdrawal method">
    In the Wallet section, choose **Crypto** (Across) or **Cash** (Meld), and enter how much USDH you want to off-ramp.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Outcome converts USDH to USDC automatically">
    The platform sells USDH on the HyperCore spot market, moves the USDC across to wherever the provider needs it, and hands off to the bridge or off-ramp. You sign one approval; the rest is composed.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Receive funds">
    For crypto: Across delivers USDC (or your chosen asset) to your destination address on the chosen chain. For cash: Meld processes the off-ramp to your bank or card.

    Settlement times depend on the provider and destination - typically minutes for Across, longer for fiat rails depending on the method.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Sending to another Hyperliquid address

If the destination is another Hyperliquid account (not an external wallet or fiat account), use the **Send** action instead. That's a direct on-chain transfer - faster than a withdrawal because it doesn't involve the bridge or any external rail.

## Fees

- **Outcome** doesn't charge a withdrawal fee.
- **Across** charges a small bridge fee (varies by source chain and amount).
- **Meld** charges a processing fee on fiat off-ramps (varies by method and region).
- **Hyperliquid** maker/taker fee applies to the USDH → USDC spot swap.

The Wallet UI shows total fees before you confirm.

<Tip>
  Check your free USDH balance in the Wallet tab before starting the withdrawal. Open orders and unconverted positions reduce free balance even if your total balance looks higher.
</Tip>

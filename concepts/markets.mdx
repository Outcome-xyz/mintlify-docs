Each market on Outcome is an outcome contract with tokens that settle to a fixed value at a known time. While the market is open, tokens trade between 0.001 and 0.999 USDH on the order book. When the market resolves, tokens for the resolved outcome settle at exactly 1 USDH; tokens for any other outcome settle at 0.

Every market is fully collateralized. The protocol holds 1 USDH for every pair of outcome tokens that exists, which is what guarantees settlement payouts without leverage or counterparty risk.

## Market types

Outcome supports binary and multi-outcome markets.

**Binary markets** have two mutually exclusive outcomes. The framing depends on the question:

- **YES / NO** for event-based markets like "will Bitcoin be above \$80,000 by tomorrow"
- **UP / DOWN** for directional markets like "will Bitcoin's price be higher at 06:00 UTC than now"

The two framings ask different questions, but the mechanics are identical: two outcome prices that sum to 1 USDH while the market is open, with exactly one settling to 1 USDH at resolution.

**Multi-outcome markets** have more than two possible outcomes, only one of which can resolve. Each outcome has its own token that pays 1 USDH if it resolves and 0 otherwise. The sum of all outcome prices stays close to 1 USDH while the market is open.

For example, a four-outcome market has four separate outcome tokens. If you hold tokens for the outcome that resolves, each token pays 1 USDH. If you hold tokens for any other outcome, they settle at 0.

## Recurring markets

Some markets are deployed automatically by the protocol on a fixed cadence. The current example is the daily BTC binary, which redeploys each day with a new target price and resolution time. When one instance settles, the next instance is already open for trading.

The contract specification (target price, expiry, underlying asset) is encoded in the protocol and applied automatically. New instances arrive without any action from a market creator.

## How markets resolve

When a market reaches its resolution time, the protocol reads the relevant data source and determines which outcome occurred. For price-based markets, this is the underlying asset's mark price on Hyperliquid at the resolution timestamp. Different market types use different data sources, and each market's specification on Outcome shows which one applies.

Once resolution is determined, settlement is automatic. Tokens for the resolved outcome become redeemable at 1 USDH each; tokens for any other outcome are worth 0. Your USDH balance updates without any action required.

## Where to find market details

Each market on Outcome includes its specification: what it resolves on, when it settles, and how the resolution is determined. For the underlying HIP-4 protocol mechanics, see the [Hyperliquid documentation](https://hyperliquid.gitbook.io/hyperliquid-docs).
